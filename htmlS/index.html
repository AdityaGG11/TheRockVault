Timeline

The Rhythm & Blues (R&B) charts in the early 50's feature music made predominately by and for black Americans. White pop artists begin covering these songs and having hits on the Pop charts. The original versions of many of these hits also begin to crossover from the R&B charts to the US Pop charts.

In 1955, Bill Haley and His Comets top the Pop Chart with the single "Rock Around The Clock". Top Music Charts are still dominated by "parents music" and rock n' roll is considered a novelty for the kids. Elvis Presley is signed to major label RCA Records, after having his contract with Sun Records bought out for $35,000.

1956, Elvis is #1 on the US charts for 25 weeks and has a record nine singles in the top 100. R&B artists continue to crossover to the pop charts and rockabilly explodes out of the south. The blurring of lines between the Pop charts and the R&B charts continues with a record number of songs.

May 1956, Johnny Cash "I Walk the Line",The third times' the charm for Sun Records artist Johnny Cash who hits #1 Country, #17 Pop with his third single. The single would go on to sell over two million copies, becoming one of Cash's best know songs.

Dec 1956 - Elvis Presley's Breakout Year 1956 goes down in music history as "the year of Elvis". Presley appears 18 times on national TV, has 4 number one singles, two number one albums, has nine singles in the Hot 100 Pop Chart at once and stars in his first hit movie - Love Me Tender.

Elvis continues to dominate the pop music charts in 1957. Sun Records continues to discover successful new artists, after losing Elvis to RCA, including the years biggest new rock and roll success Jerry Lee Lewis. Rock & Roll grows internationally as Bill Haley and His Comets complete a successful world tour.

Tommy Steele become Britain's first homegrown rock star, topping the UK charts with his cover of Guy Mitchell's "Singing the Blues". Mitchell's version of the song would also reach #1 in both the UK and US.

Mar 1957 - Elvis Presley buys Graceland Mansion Riding the top of the Pop charts, Elvis Presley takes time out to purchase the Graceland mansion in Memphis, in part to provide more security and to avoid neighbors complaints about his fans.

Billboard introduces the US Hot 100 Singles, a chart that combines both store sales and radio airplay. Elvis' career is interrupted when he is drafted into the Army and serves in Germany for two years. Cliff Richard hits #2 on the UK Charts with "Move It", considered to be the first rock and roll hit written and produced outside the U.S.

Mar 1958 - Chuck Berry "Sweet Little Sixteen" St. Louis, Missouri singer, songwriter, guitarist Chuck Berry has his biggest hit yet with the single "Sweet Little Sixteen" #1 US R&B, # 2 US Pop and #16 UK. Berry would score again in May with "Johnny B. Goode" #2 US R&B and #8 US Pop.

1959, a plane crash in Clear Lake, Iowa kills three of rock music's biggest stars, Buddy Holly, The Big Bopper and Ritchie Valens. Tamla Records, which eventually becomes Motown, is founded in Detroit by Berry Gordy. As the fifties come to a close Rock and Roll accounts for 43 percent of all records sold.

Dec 1959, As the fifties come to a close, Rock and Roll music accounts for 43% of all records sold and the its' influence on teen culture has spread to movies, television, fashion, attitudes and everyday language

1960, Teen tragedy songs are popular. In a real life tragedy, American rock star Eddie Cochran is killed in a UK car accident. Elvis Presley returns from military service in Germany and continues to score hits with a less rock, more "adult" sound.

1961, US regional music scenes develop unique styles and sounds. Surf music emerges from Southern California. While girl groups and the Brill Building sound emanate from New York. Berry Gordy's Tamla / Motown label has it's first number one hit.

1962, Elvis gives up touring to focus on acting. The Beatles are rejected by Decca Records, but later signed to EMI. The Mashed Potato, The Twist and the Watusi are popular as dance crazes continue to sweep the US.

Dec 1963, Beatlemania takes hold in the UK , while in the US The Beatles remain unknown. Motown continues to bring the hits, including the youngest person to hit #1 in the US with Little Stevie Wonder at 13-years old. Surf music and the girl group sound are at their zenith.

1964, Beatlemania hits the US as the UK group scores six number one hits in the US in 1964. Televison is influential with new primetime music shows, and The Beatles first Ed Sullivan appearance breaking TV ratings records.

September 1964, Texas singer, songwriter Roy Orbison tops the charts for the second time in both the US & UK with the single "Oh Pretty Woman". It would be Orbison's seventh and final US Top Ten hit single

1965, Folk music favorite Bob Dylan goes electric. The hits keep coming from Motown with the Supremes having three more #1 singles, plus #1 hits from The Temptations and The Four Tops. The Beatles play the largest concert to date in front of 55,600 fans at New York City's Shea Stadium. The British Invasion keeps delivering with hits.

1967, The Beatles release the album Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band. Paul Mc Cartney reveals that all four Beatles have dropped acid. The debut album from the Jimi Hendrix Experience is released. California band The Turtles return to the pop charts with their biggest single "Happy Together" #1 US, #12 UK.

May 1967, Elvis Presley marries 21-year-old Priscilla Beaulieu in Las Vegas. Having met in Germany while Elvis was serving in the Army, they had been dating since she was fourteen.

1968, Hard rock becomes more prevalent as it morphs into heavy metal. The rock musical Hair opens on Broadway. The Beatles, Donovan, Beach Boys singer Mike Love, actress Mia Farrow and others travel to India to hang with Maharishi Mahesh Yogi.

June 1968, Johnny Cash records two live performances at California's Folsom Prison in January with support from June Carter and Carl Perkins. The resulting live album released in May goes to #1 on the US Country chart and revitalizes Cash's career.

December 1968, NBC-TV airs the Elvis comeback special - featuring Presley and his original 1950's era backing band playing live, mostly acoustic versions of his own songs and other favorites in front of a studio audience. The show is a ratings hit and helps revive Presley's career.

1969, The Beatles perform live for the last time on the roof of their London record label Apple Records. Elvis Presley returns to live performances and hits #1 on the US charts after a seven year absence. UK hard rock band Led Zeppelin release their self titled debut album which goes Top Ten in both the US and UK.

February 19670, The self-titled debut album from Birmingham, UK band Black Sabbath is released and charts at #8 UK, #23 US despite poor reviews. With early success the band records and releases their second album .

Feb 1970, Paul McCartney files a petition with the court to dissolve The Beatles partnership. He is angry with the group's decision to hire Phil Spector to remix the songs that would become the Let It Be album, along with other disagreements about Apple Records.

Jan 1971, UK singer and pianist Elton John has his first Top Ten hit with "Your Song". Both John and lyricist Bernie Taupin had been staff songwriters for Dick James DJM Records writing songs for other artists

Jan 1971, New Orleans, Louisiana singer songwriter King Floyd hits #1 US R&B, #6 US Pop and #41 UK with his composition "Groove Me".

Nov 1971, Former Beatle John Lennon has the biggest hit single of his solo career with "Imagine" reaching #3 US. The song was not released as a single in the UK until after Lennon's murder in 1980 when it hit #1 UK for four weeks.

Nov 1971, Led Zeppelin release their fourth album which tops the UK album chart and reaches #2 US. The album contains the bands best known song "Stairway To Heaven" and becomes one of the best selling albums of all time.

1972, The popularity of Glam/Glitter rock continues to grow in the UK with hits from T-Rex, Gary Glitter, Slade, Alice Cooper, The Sweet and David Bowie. Fifties rock and roll pioneers make a comeback with Elvis Presley, Rick Nelson and Chuck Berry all scoring hits.

1973, A January 14th Elvis Presley concert in Honolulu, Hawaii is beamed live via satellite to over 40 countries in Europe and Asia. With an audience estimated at over a billion, it is not shown live in the US due to Super Bowl VII taking place the same day.

July 1973, UK progressive rock band Queen release their self titled debut album. Despite positive reviews, the album peaks at #24 UK, while only reaching #77 in the US. The album's lead single "Keep Yourself Alive" fails to catch on and doesn't chart in the UK or US.

July 1973, The May 5th appearance of UK band Led Zeppelin at Florida's Tampa Stadium breaks the Beatles record for largest stadium concert audience at 56,800. Filming occurs at several concerts on this tour for later use in the film The Song Remains The Same.

September 1973, Upper Darby, Pennsylvania singer, songwriter Jim Croce is killed in a plane crash along with other band members while touring to support his fourth album Life and Times. Croce's song "Time In A Bottle" would hit #1 US in December.

1974, Bob Dylan hits the road for the first time since 1966 backed by The Band. The first Knebworth Festival in Hertfordshire, England attracts 60,000 fans with headliners the Allman Brothers. In the US the California Jam attracts over 300,000 fans for Deep Purple, Emerson, Lake & Palmer, and Black Sabbath plus others.

March 1974, UK progressive rock band Queen release their second album Queen II which reaches #5 UK, #49 US and spawns a UK Top Ten single with "Seven Seasons Of Rhye". In November their third album is released and the single "Killer Queen" hits #2 UK, #12 US.

Sep 1975, After hearing from fans that their studio albums didn't capture the sound of their live shows, New York City rock band Kiss respond with their double disc live album Alive! and out-sell their three previous studio albums combined. The album reaches #9 US, #49 UK

Nov 1975, UK rock band Queen have their first UK #1 single with "Bohemian Rhapsody" (#9 US in 1976). Written by lead singer Freddie Mercury in a departure from the traditional pop formula, the semi-operatic song consists of six sections without a chorus.

1976, Disco songs continue to impact the singles charts, while punk rock emerges in both the US and UK. The Rolling Stones concert at Knebworth UK draws 120,000 fans. Elton John discloses his bisexuality for the first time in a Rolling Stone magazine interview.

Aug 1977, Rock pioneer Elvis Presley is found dead at home at age 42. Two days later 80,000 people line the streets to see his funeral procession to Forest Hills Cemetery. His Memphis home, Graceland, remains a major tourist attraction to this day.

Oct 1977, Johnsburg, Pennslyvania native Domenico Monardo, known professionally as Meco goes to US #1, #7 UK with his spacey disco single "Star Wars Theme/Cantina Band". Helped by the success of the Star Wars film, the single sells over a million copies.

Dec 1977, The low budget film Saturday Night Fever, starring John Travolta as a working class disco dancer, is released to box office success driven by the film's soundtrack LP. Featuring the Bee Gees, the album would become the best selling soundtrack ever.

Feb 1978, 19-year-old UK singer songwriter Kate Bush releases her debut album "The Kick Inside" which reaches #3 UK. Her debut single "Wuthering Heights" hits #1 UK in March for four weeks. Bush goes on to release ten albums, all Top Ten in the UK.

Feb 1978, Pasadena, California hard rock band Van Halen release their self-titled debut album which reaches #19 US, #34 UK. Eddie Van Halen's unique guitar style showcased on the instrumental track "Eruption", along with steady touring gain the band a following.

April 1978, Australian brothers The Bee Gees spend five weeks with the top two spots on the US Hot 100 with "Night Fever" #1 and "Stayin' Alive" #2. Both songs are from the Saturday Night Fever Soundtrack album which goes on to sell over 45 million copies.

March 1979, UK progressive rock band Supertramp release their sixth album Breakfast In America and reach #1 US, #3 UK. The album includes two US Top Ten singles "The Logical Song" #6 US, #7 UK and "Take The Long Way Home" #10 US.

May 1979, Disco music continues to demonstrate it's chart dominance in May when eight of the US Top Ten hits are disco songs including #1 "Hot Stuff" from Donna Summer. Summer would go on to have three #1 singles in 1979, which would be disco's peak year for sales.

July 1979, Memphis, Tennessee R&B singer Anita Ward scores big with her first single "Ring My Bell" which tops both the US and UK charts. This single would be Ward's only Top 40 hit. Ward's debut album Songs Of Love reaches #8 US.

July 1979, Australian hard rock band AC/DC break into the US Top 20 and UK Top 10 with their seventh album Highway to Hell. It would be the final album with lead singer and contributing songwriter Bon Scott who dies of acute alcohol poisoning in Feb 1980.

Oct 1979, American singer Michael Jackson has his second #1 US hit single as a solo artist with "Don't Stop 'Til You Get Enough" #3 UK. The song is from Jackson's fifth solo album Off The Wall #3 US, and is his first collaboration with producer Quincy Jones.

Jan 1980, American singer Michael Jackson has his second #1 US hit from his 1979 album 'Off The Wall' when his single "Rock With You" hits #1 US, #1 US R&B and #7 UK. The song is Jackson's third #1 hit as a solo artist and is the first of his many 1980's chart toppers.

April 1980, The self titled debut album from East London heavy metal band Iron Maiden is released and charts at #4 UK. Two singles from the album "Running Free" and "Sanctuary" are released and break into the lower reaches of the UK Top 40.

Sep 1980, Led Zeppelin drummer John Bonham is found dead in his home from pulmonary aspiration following a bout of heavy drinking. Rather than find a new drummer, Led Zeppelin decides to call it quits after eight studio albums, including seven US/UK #1's.

Dec 1980, On December 8th John Lennon is shot to death outside his Manhattan apartment by a deranged fan. Earlier that day Lennon had signed an autograph for the gunman. Lennon had just released his first album in five years - Double Fantasy in November.

Jan 1981, The album Double Fantasy by John Lennon and Yoko Ono jumps to #1 in both the US and UK following Lennon's death in December. The single "(Just Like) Starting Over" also tops the US and UK charts, as does the album's second single "Woman".

March 1981, American country singer Dolly Parton hits #1 on the US Top 100 with her single "9 To 5" from the movie of the same name. The movie featuring Jane Fonda and Lily Tomlin is also Parton's film debut. The song is Parton's thirteenth #1 US Country single.

May 1981, Jamaican reggae pioneer Bob Marley dies of melanoma in Miami. Marley and his band The Wailers began their recording career in Jamaica in 1965 and released eleven studio albums, four of which reached the UK Top Ten, along with one US Top Ten album.

Aug 1981, MTV (Music Television) launches its 24-hour a day music cable channel on 300 US cable TV systems. By 1983 MTV is available on 2,000 US cable systems and the use of promotional music videos is influencing radio play and the music charts.

Nov 1981, UK rock band Queen team up with David Bowie for the single "Under Pressure" which reaches #1 UK, #29 US. It is Queen's second #1 single and would be included on the band's 1982 album Hot Space #4 UK, #22 US. It is Bowie's third UK #1 single.

1982, The "Second British Invasion" emerges with numerous UK synth bands scoring hits in the U.S. The first US Festival is held drawing over 300,000 people to see The Police, Tom Petty and Fleetwood Mac among others. Michael Jackson's album Thriller is released, going on to become the best selling album of all time.

1983, Michael Jackson unveils the moonwalk dance move on the Motown 25th Anniversary TV Special. MTV music video plays drive sales of singles and heavily influences radio play. The band KISS begin performing without their make-up.

1984, The first MTV Video Music Awards are held in New York City and Madonna's suggestive performance of "Like A Virgin" draws attention. Motown star Marvin Gaye is shot and killed in an argument with his father. Prince releases the soundtrack for Purple Rain which charts at #1 US for 24 weeks.

1985, MTV launches a sister station, VH1, with a more classic rock sound targeted at an older age group. 40 artists gather to record "We Are The World" for African famine relief. The Live Aid concert for famine relief is held simultaneously in London and Philadelphia and broadcast live worldwide.

1986, The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame holds it's first induction ceremony honoring eight rock music pioneers. In May Cleveland, Ohio is selected as the site for the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Museum.

1987, Rap music continues to grow in popularity. Whitney Houston becomes the first female artist to debut at #1 with her second album. Aretha Franklin is the first female artist inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

June 1987, Guns N' Roses, a band from the Los Angeles' Sunset Strip hard rock scene, release their debut album Appetite For Destruction to poor reviews The album begins a slow climb up the charts, reaching US #1 over a year later and going on to sell over 30 million copies worldwide.

Oct 1987, Michael Jackson reaches #1 US, #3 UK with the title track from his seventh album Bad. The album goes #1 in both the US and UK and sells over ten million copies in the US alone.

In 1988, CD's outsell vinyl albums for the first time, but cassette tapes remain as the top selling medium for music. Atlantic Records holds a 40th Anniversary concert at Madison Square Garden with many past and present label artists including a reunited Led Zeppelin. Rock and Roll pioneer Roy Orbison dies

1989, Janet Jackson and Bobby Brown continue to drive the growth of New Jack Swing, which merges hip hop with R&B. Michael Jackson starts referring to himself as "KING OF POP".

1990, Female artists dominate the top of the pop charts. Jimi Hendrix's Stratocaster guitar from Woodstock is sold for $295,000. Roger Waters and guest stars perform The Wall in Berlin, Germany just eight months after the fall of the Berlin Wall.

March 1991, Wicked Game, California singer songwriter Chris Isaak's ninth single is his first to chart, reaching #6 US, #10 UK after being used in the David Lynch film Wild At Heart. The song is alsob helped by the stylish and steamy Herb Ritts.

July 1991, Canadian singer songwriter Bryan Adams has his second US and UK #1 single with "(Everything I Do) I Do It For You". The song spends a record sixteen weeks atop the UK singles chart and seven weeks at #1 in the US.

Sep 1991Los Angeles hard rock band Guns N' Roses release their third and fourth albums Use Your Illusion l and ll. The albums chart at #2 and #1 respectively in both the US and UK. The single "November Rain" hits #3 US, #4 UK.

Nov 1991, Freddie Mercury, flamboyant lead singer of the UK rock band Queen, dies from complications related to AIDS. Mercury's final vocal tracks would be released on the Queen's last album Made In Heaven released in 1995.

Nov 1991, American rapper and songwriter Tupac Shakur (stage name 2Pac) releases his debut album '2Pacalypse' Now on Interscope Records. The album peaks at #64 US and #13 US R&B/Hip Hop on it's way to going Gold.

Jan 1992Aberdeen, Washington band Nirvana bring alternative and grunge rock into the Top Ten with the single "Smells Like Teen Spirit" hitting #6 US and #7 UK. The video, featuring the band playing at a high school pep rally, is considered one of the best music videos ever.

March 1992, Veteran UK singer/songwriter and guitarist Eric Clapton has the biggest single of his career with the acoustic ballad "Tears In Heaven" #2 US, #5 UK. The lyrics are influenced by the death of his 4-year-old son Conor.

Feb 1993, West coast rapper 2Pac releases his second album Strictly For My N.I.G.G.A.Z. on Interscope Records and charts at #4 R&B/Hip Hop and #24 US Billboard 200 on it's way to going US Platinum.

Nov 1993, Grunge stars Nirvana play the Sony Music Studios in NYC for a recording of MTV Unplugged. Rather than covering their hits, the band plays lesser known cuts and covers from David Bowie.

Nov 1993, West Coast rapper and Dr. Dre protoge Snoop Doggy Dogg releases his first album 'Doggystyle' which debut's at #1 US and reaches #12 UK. The album's sound is influenced by the Parliment-Funkadelic funk sound.

Jan 1994, 'Under The Pink', the second album from Maryland singer songwriter Tori Amos, debuts at #1 UK and goes to #12 US. Amos could play music she only heard once and has described seeing music in colors - known as chromesthesia.

April 1994, On April 8th Kurt Cobain(27), of grunge band Nirvana, is found dead of a self-inflicted gunshot wound in his Seattle home. The singer songwriter had struggled with depression, heroin addiction and chronic health problems for several years.

June 1995, American brother/sister superstars Michael and Janet Jackson team up for the single "Scream" which charts at #5 US, #3 UK. The video for the song costs $7 Million and is one of the most expensive ever produced.

Jan 1996, The surviving members of The Beatles George Harrison, Ringo Starr and Paul McCartney add vocals and instrumentation to a John Lennon home demo and release the single "Free As A Bird".

Jun 1996, Having performed without makeup since 1983, rock band Kiss appear at the Grammy's in February with their original lineup in full makeup. The reunited group launch a tour at Tiger Stadium in Detroit in front of 40,000 fans.

June 1996, Brooklyn, New York rapper Jay-Z starts his own label Rock-A-Fella Records to release his first album Reasonable Doubt #23 US, #3 US Hip Hop. Despite the lack of a radio hit, the album goes Platinum in the US.

1997 is a massive year for female artists, both on the charts and in concert tours. Sarah MacLachlan founds the Lilith Fair tour featuring all female artists. Ozzy Osbourne launches the first Ozzfest Tour. Janet Jackson signs a record $80 million recording contract.

Feb 1998, Celine Dion's love theme for the blockbuster film Titanic "My Heart Will Go On" goes #1 in both the US and UK, on its' way to becoming one of the best selling singles of all time with worldwide sales of over 18 million copies. The song wins an Oscar, a Golden Globe and several Grammy awards.

Nov 1998, American singer and actor Cher returns to the charts with the dance single "Believe" #1 UK and hitting #1 US in 1999. Selling over 10 million copies, the single features an early use of Auto-Tune for a vocal distortion effect that is commonly used today.

Jan 1999, McComb, Mississippi actor and singer Britney Spears, a former cast member of The Mickey Mouse Club, hit's #1 in the US and UK with her debut single "...Baby One More Time". The song is the title track from her debut album which hits #1 US, #2 UK and sells 25 million copies worldwide.

Feb 1999, Detroit, Michigan based rapper Eminem (Marshall Mathers) his major label debut album The Slim Shady LP #2 US, #10 UK. Produced by Dr. Dre and others, the album goes 4X Platinum in the US and #X Platinum UK.

Early Rock and Roll

Rock and Roll is a genre of popular music that evolved in the United States during the late 1940s and early 1950s. It originated from African-American music such as jazz, rhythm and blues, boogie-woogie, electric blues, gospel, and jump blues, as well as country music.
While rock and roll's formative elements can be heard in blues records from the 1920s and in country records of the 1930s, the genre did not acquire its name until 1954. Rock is a broad genre of popular music that originated from "Rock and Roll". Rock was the most popular genre of music in the U.S. and much of the Western world from the 1950s to the 2010s. "Rockabilly" usually refers to the type of rock and roll music which was played and recorded in the mid-1950s primarily by white singers such as Elvis Presley, Carl Perkins, Johnny Cash, and Jerry Lee Lewis, who drew mainly on the country roots of the music. Presley popularized rock and roll on a wider scale than any other single performer and by 1956, he had emerged as the singing sensation of the nation. In 1956, the arrival of rockabilly was underlined by the success of songs like "Folsom Prison Blues" by Johnny Cash, "Blue Suede Shoes" by Perkins, and the No. 1 hit "Heartbreak Hotel" by Presley. Commentators have traditionally perceived a decline of rock and roll in the late 1950s and early 1960s. By 1959, the death of Buddy Holly, the Big Bopper and Ritchie Valens in a plane crash, the departure of Elvis for the army.

British Invasion

In the sixties rock music fully completes it's takeover of the popular music charts, with only a minority of albums or songs outside the genre having a major chart impact. Elvis Presley continues to score hits in the early part of the decade, but rock music continues to diversify with the folk revival, the Brill Building sound, Phil Spector's wall of sound, girl groups and surf music, all impacting the early part of the decade. "I Want to Hold Your Hand" was the Beatles' first number one hit on the Billboard Hot 100, spending seven weeks at the top and a total of 15 weeks on the chart.
Beatlemania hits the US as the UK group scores six number one US hits in 1964. Televison is influential with new primetime music shows, and The Beatles first Ed Sullivan appearance breaking TV ratings records.The British Invasion begins as more UK rock acts follow The Beatles to US chart success. Hence, By 1964 American artists were sharing the top of the charts with U.K. bands led by the Beatles and The Rolling Stones. Soon these groups were composing their own material, combining US forms of music and infusing it with a high energy beat. The British Invasion helped internationalize the production of rock and roll, opening the door for subsequent British (and Irish) performers to achieve international success. It dented the careers of established R&B acts like Fats Domino and Chubby Checker and even temporarily derailed the chart success of surviving rock and roll acts, including Elvis.

Golden Age of Rock

The Beatles break up in 1970, but all four members continue to impact the decade with successful solo careers. The early seventies are marked by the deaths of Jimi Hendrix, Janis Joplin and Jim Morrison who all die at the age of 27.
The decade is often referred to as the “Golden Age of Rock” due to the incredible number of iconic and influential bands that emerged, including Led Zeppelin, Pink Floyd, The Who, The Rolling Stones, Aerosmith, and Queen. Nostalgia for rock's earlier days grows in the seventies. Compilation albums of various artists and musical styles are popular. The 1970s are regarded as a pivotal decade in rock music history, marked by innovation, creativity, and a sense of rebellion, which laid the groundwork for the diverse range of rock styles that followed. Rock concerts move from theaters and clubs, to sports arenas. The 1970s were known for their epic live shows, with many bands pushing the boundaries of concert production, sound, and lighting. July 1973, Led Zeppelin play to 56,800 at Tampa Stadium, breaking the Beatles Shea Stadium record for largest concert attendance. Elvis Presley dies suddenly in 1977 at age 42. Huge crowds begin flocking to his Memphis home, Graceland, which continues to be a tourist destination to this day. By the end of the 70's, Disco dominated the singles charts and many rock acts score with disco style hits, including Blondie, Rod Stewart, Electric Light Orchestra, Kenny Rogers and even Kiss.

The Rise of MTV

1980s was a transformative era for rock music, marked by the rise of new subgenres, technological innovations, and a global explosion of popularity. John Lennon is shot to death by a fan in 1980 just as he was coming back into the public eye with a new album. Led Zeppelin decides to call it quits after drummer John Bonham is found dead in his home from pulmonary aspiration.
MTV plays music videos 24 hours a day and provides daily rock news clips. MTV (Music Television) is launched on 300 U.S. cable TV systems in 1981. By 1983 MTV is available on 2,000 cable systems. VH1 is launched in 1984 with a more classic rock format. It's marketing tool is influential in bringing numerous new music styles into the mainstream, including a resurgence in heavy metal, the emergence of synthpop, and the growth in new wave, rap and hip-hop.Recordings and concerts to benefit charity are supported by many music artists including Band Aid, plus the Live Aid and Farm Aid concerts.
The “rock n roll lifestyle” became a defining feature of the decade, with many bands like U2, Bruce Springsteen, and Guns N' Roses embracing the party-hard ethos. Rap music continues to grow in popularity. Aretha Franklin is the first female artist inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

Mainstream Success

In the 90's Hip-Hop continues to expand in influence and grow in diversity. With huge growth in sales, radio airplay and overall success for artists. Alternative Rock and it's sub-genres Grunge and Pop Punk expand in popularity and ironically, explode into the mainstream during the 1990's.
Many alternative bands, including REM, The Smashing Pumpkins, Red Hot Chilli Peppers, The Smiths and laterb grunge bands like Nirvana, Soundgarden, Pearl Jam and others find success with mainstream audiences. Heavy metal morphs into new sub-genres such as rap metal or rapcore, nu metal and industrial metal. Electronic music continues to change as well, with synthpop, techno and house splitting into new styles including trance, drums & bass, trip-hop and eurodance. Pop and teen-pop continue to appeal to a younger radio audience.
Singer / Songwriters enjoy a resurgence especially for female artists like Norah Jones, Sarah McLachlan, Alanis Morissette, Tori Amos, Jewel, Natalie Merchant and Sheryl Crow. Advancements in computers and software allow for digital music creation and recording on home computers. The internet and sound compression allow for digital distribution of music, often bypassing the need for a major label album release.
