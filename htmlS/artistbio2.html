The King of Pop

Michael Joseph Jackson (August 29, 1958 – June 25, 2009) was an American singer, songwriter, dancer, and philanthropist.
Dubbed the "King of Pop", he is regarded as one of the most significant cultural figures of the 20th century.
He revolutionized pop culture and reigned over the industry as the most popular artist until his death in 2009 from an overdose of propofol administered by his personal physician, Conrad Murray.

Dangerous (1991)
